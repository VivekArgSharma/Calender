Theme 3 - Music Scene Mapping

Goal
- Keep a clean center wall for the calendar.
- Make the music scene feel intentional and believable with the available assets.
- Use side framing and simple support props where needed so the composition feels complete.

Calendar-safe zone
- Reserve the middle 45-50% of the back wall for the calendar.
- Keep all tall silhouettes out of this middle section.
- Let the scene energy happen on the side walls and floor edges.

Asset placement
- Guitar Amp: place on the right floor near the wall as the main anchor object.
- Electric guitar: place leaning against the amp at a slight diagonal; allow a little crop at the right edge if it helps preserve center space.
- Midi controller: place on the left side on a very simple stand or shelf made from basic geometry so it does not look dropped on the floor.
- Saxophone: place on the left side as a secondary accent, ideally on a small stand or wall mount, positioned higher than the midi controller.

Support props
- Use simple geometric support props if needed: thin stand legs, a small shelf, or a narrow pedestal.
- Keep support props minimal and neutral so they help the music assets feel natural without stealing attention.

Composition notes
- The amp and guitar create the heavier visual mass on the right.
- The midi controller and saxophone create a layered musical vignette on the left.
- The center wall stays open and becomes the hero area for the calendar.
- Do not cluster all music items on one side; the left-right split gives the calendar room to breathe.

Camera and staging
- Use a mostly front-facing camera with slight depth.
- Keep the amp lower in frame and the saxophone slightly higher so the left side does not feel too flat.
- If an object needs cropping, crop at the outer edges rather than moving it toward the calendar.

Lighting
- Keep the center wall softly illuminated.
- Add warmer or more dramatic side accents to the music props so the scene feels expressive without overpowering the calendar.

Visual intent
- The final scene should feel like a curated music corner framing the calendar wall, with enough personality to stand apart from the other themes.
