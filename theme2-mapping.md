Theme 2 - Art Scene Mapping

Goal
- Keep the center wall open for the calendar.
- Make the room feel like a creative studio with balanced objects on both sides.
- Use the taller art object on one side and lower furniture weight on the other.

Calendar-safe zone
- Reserve the middle 45-50% of the back wall for the calendar.
- Keep the top and sides of the calendar clear from the easel silhouette.
- Any strong props should live in the left and right thirds of the scene.

Asset placement
- Easel: place on the left side of the floor, angled slightly toward the center but kept outside the calendar zone.
- Paint Can: place near the easel base, slightly forward and low, so it adds detail without visual clutter.
- Drafting Table: place on the right side, lower than the easel in visual importance and angled inward.
- Wall painting: place on the upper right wall above or just behind the drafting table, but still outside the center wall area.

Composition notes
- The easel should be the dominant art prop and create the main identity for the theme.
- The drafting table should balance the left side without becoming heavier than the easel.
- The wall painting adds vertical interest on the right while keeping the center wall empty.
- Leave enough negative space around the calendar so it feels mounted on the room's main wall.

Camera and staging
- Keep the camera mostly straight-on.
- Allow a little depth so the easel and drafting table do not feel flat.
- If needed, crop a small portion of the drafting table or easel at the side edges rather than letting them drift toward the center.

Lighting
- Use a soft studio-like wash in the center.
- Add subtle side lighting to pull shape from the easel and drafting table.
- Let the wall painting catch a touch of highlight so it reads as mounted decor.

Visual intent
- The final look should feel like an art studio built around the calendar wall, not a room where the calendar is squeezed in afterward.
