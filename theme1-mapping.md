Theme 1 - Tech Scene Mapping

Goal
- Keep a large, clean center wall for the calendar.
- Make the room feel like a styled tech workspace without crowding the middle.
- Use side-weighted composition so the calendar remains the hero element.

Calendar-safe zone
- Reserve the middle 45% of the back wall for the calendar.
- Do not place any model so it overlaps the calendar silhouette.
- Keep stronger shadows and detail on the left and right edges, not in the center.

Asset placement
- Adjustable Desk: place on the ground on the right side, pushed close to the right wall.
- Macintosh Classic: place on top of the desk, angled slightly toward the center; only 60-75% of it should be visible so it feels naturally cropped by the right edge of the screen.
- Wall Art 02: place on the upper left wall, high enough to balance the Mac visually.
- Picture frame: place below the wall art on the left wall, with enough space between both pieces so they read as two separate decor elements.

Composition notes
- The desk and computer should create the main visual mass on the right.
- The two wall pieces should create a lighter vertical stack on the left.
- The center wall should remain open and calm so the calendar can sit there clearly.
- Avoid bringing any object too close to the center; leave breathing room around the calendar.

Camera and staging
- Use a mostly front-facing camera with only a slight angle for depth.
- Keep the calendar centered in world space, not just centered in the viewport.
- Let the right-side desk sit slightly lower in frame so it supports the calendar instead of competing with it.

Lighting
- Keep the center wall softly lit and clean.
- Add slightly stronger contrast on the desk/computer side and mild accent light on the left wall decor.

Visual intent
- The result should feel like a retro-modern workspace framing a physical wall calendar.
